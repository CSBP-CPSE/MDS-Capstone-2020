Ontario COVID-19 Map

Instructions
  • Scroll in the map to zoom in and out.
  • Click and drag the map to move.
  • Hover over a public health unit or long-term care home (represented as points) to view details.
The chloropleth map of Ontario (left) displays the proportion of cases for each of the 36 Public Health Units (PHUs). To calculate this proportion, the number of cases for each PHU was determined from data provided by Statistics Canada and divided by the population of the corresponding PHU. On hover, additional data is provided regarding the PHU name, count of fatalities, and Amenity Score, which represents a measure of "connectedness" according to proximity to amenities (transit, pharmacies, education, employment... etc.).

Amenity richness was calculated from an amenity score provided by Statistics Canada. Each dissemination block receives a score of 0 or 1, representing low or high proximity to amenities, respectively, and the mean of dissemination block scores for a single PHU was calculated. The distribution of amenity-richness scores seems to reflect the proportion of COVID-19 on the PHU level with highly connected PHUs reporting high case proportions and amenity-poor PHUs reporting lower proportions. Factor analysis supported this observation.
Zoom and hover over the map of Ontario Long-Term Care homes (right) to view the home name, type, number of beds, associated Local Health Integration Network (LHIN), and outbreak status. In this map, Ontario is subdivided according to LHIN.


Data sources: Data from Statistics Canada - proximity data, Ontario Covid-19 data and health data.
Authors: Ngan Lyle, Sofia Bahtmutsky, Kaitlyn Hobbs, Shreeram Murali.
